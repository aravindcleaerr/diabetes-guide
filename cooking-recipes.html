Diabetes-Friendly Recipes

Delicious, blood-sugar-friendly recipes for every meal of the day

🍳 Breakfast Recipes

1. Oats Upma

Approx. per serving: 28 g carbs • 8 g protein • 6 g fat • 200 kcal

  • 1 cup rolled oats (not instant)
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds, ½ tsp urad dal, curry leaves
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • ½ cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas)
  • 1 green chilli, chopped
  • ½ tsp turmeric, salt to taste
  • 1 tsp oil (mustard or olive)
  • Juice of ½ lemon

Method: Dry-roast oats on medium heat for 2–3 minutes until lightly golden; set aside. Heat oil, add mustard seeds, urad dal, and curry leaves. When they splutter, add onion and green chilli; sauté until translucent. Add vegetables and turmeric; cook for 3–4 minutes. Add 1.5 cups water and salt; bring to a boil. Add roasted oats, stir well, cover, and cook on low for 2 minutes. Finish with lemon juice. Serves 2.

2. Ragi Dosa

Approx. per serving (2 dosas): 35 g carbs • 6 g protein • 4 g fat • 200 kcal

  • 1 cup ragi (finger millet) flour
  • ¼ cup rice flour
  • ¼ cup finely chopped onion
  • 1 green chilli, finely chopped
  • Fresh coriander, cumin seeds, salt to taste
  • Water to make batter

Method: Mix ragi flour, rice flour, onion, chilli, coriander, cumin, and salt. Add water gradually to make a thin dosa batter (thinner than regular dosa batter since ragi doesn’t spread easily). Heat a non-stick tawa, pour a ladleful of batter, and spread into a circle. Drizzle a few drops of oil around the edges. Cook until the bottom is crispy, flip briefly, and serve with coconut chutney and sambar. Makes 4–5 dosas.

3. Vegetable Poha

Approx. per serving: 32 g carbs • 5 g protein • 6 g fat • 200 kcal

  • 1 cup thick poha (flattened rice)
  • 1 tsp oil, mustard seeds, curry leaves, peanuts (10–12)
  • ½ cup mixed veggies (onion, capsicum, carrot, green peas)
  • ½ tsp turmeric, salt, juice of ½ lemon

Method: Rinse poha in water, drain, and set aside for 5 minutes. Heat oil, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, and peanuts. Add vegetables and turmeric; sauté for 3 minutes. Add drained poha, salt, and mix gently. Cook for 2 minutes on low heat. Finish with lemon juice and fresh coriander. Serves 2.

4. Moong Dal Chilla (Savoury Pancakes)

Approx. per serving (2 chillas): 22 g carbs • 12 g protein • 4 g fat • 170 kcal

  • 1 cup moong dal, soaked 4–6 hours
  • 1 green chilli, small piece of ginger
  • ¼ cup finely chopped onion, coriander leaves
  • Salt, ¼ tsp cumin powder

Method: Grind soaked moong dal with chilli, ginger, and minimal water into a thick, smooth batter. Mix in onion, coriander, salt, and cumin. Heat a non-stick pan, pour a ladleful and spread into a thin circle. Cook on medium heat until the bottom is golden, flip, and cook briefly. Serve with green chutney or plain curd. Makes 5–6 chillas.

🍛 Lunch Recipes

5. Quinoa Vegetable Pulao

Approx. per serving: 38 g carbs • 10 g protein • 7 g fat • 260 kcal

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed well
  • 1 tsp ghee or olive oil
  • 1 bay leaf, 2 cloves, 1 small cinnamon stick, 2 cardamom pods
  • 1 small onion (sliced), 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (beans, carrots, peas, cauliflower)
  • Salt, ½ tsp garam masala, ¼ tsp turmeric
  • 2 cups water

Method: Heat ghee in a pressure cooker or pot. Add whole spices, then onion; sauté until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute. Add vegetables, turmeric, and garam masala; stir for 2 minutes. Add quinoa and water; mix well. Pressure cook for 1 whistle or cover and simmer for 15 minutes until water is absorbed. Fluff with a fork. Serves 3.

6. Mixed Millet Khichdi

Approx. per serving: 40 g carbs • 12 g protein • 5 g fat • 250 kcal

  • ½ cup foxtail millet + ¼ cup little millet (or any two millets)
  • ½ cup moong dal, washed
  • 1 tsp ghee, cumin seeds, asafoetida (hing)
  • ½ tsp turmeric, salt
  • ½ cup chopped vegetables (lauki, carrot, tomato)
  • 3 cups water

Method: Wash millets and dal together. Heat ghee, add cumin and hing. Add vegetables and turmeric; sauté briefly. Add millets, dal, water, and salt. Pressure cook for 3 whistles or simmer in a pot for 25 minutes until soft and porridge-like. Serve with a dollop of curd and pickle. Serves 2.

7. Palak Dal with Brown Rice

Approx. per serving (dal + ½ cup brown rice): 42 g carbs • 14 g protein • 5 g fat • 270 kcal

  • 1 cup masoor or moong dal
  • 2 cups chopped spinach (palak)
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 tsp ghee, cumin seeds, garlic (3–4 cloves), dried red chilli
  • ½ tsp turmeric, salt, ½ tsp garam masala

Method: Cook dal with turmeric and salt until soft (3 cups water). Blanch spinach briefly, blend roughly. In a pan, heat ghee, add cumin, garlic, and dried chilli. Add tomato; cook until soft. Add cooked dal and blended spinach; simmer for 5–7 minutes. Add garam masala. Serve with ½ cup brown rice. Serves 3.

🥗 Dinner Recipes

8. Grilled Fish / Chicken with Roasted Vegetables

Approx. per serving: 12 g carbs • 30 g protein • 8 g fat • 240 kcal

  • 150 g fish fillet (basa, salmon, or rohu) or chicken breast
  • Marinade: 1 tbsp curd, ½ tsp turmeric, ½ tsp red chilli powder, ½ tsp garam masala, salt, lemon juice
  • Vegetables: 1 cup broccoli, bell pepper, zucchini, mushrooms
  • 1 tsp olive oil, salt, pepper, dried herbs

Method: Marinate fish/chicken for at least 30 minutes. Toss vegetables with olive oil, salt, pepper, and herbs. Grill or bake the protein at 200°C for 15–20 minutes (fish) or 25–30 minutes (chicken). Roast vegetables alongside or on a separate tray for 20 minutes. Serve together with a lemon wedge. Serves 1.

9. Lauki (Bottle Gourd) Soup

Approx. per serving: 10 g carbs • 3 g protein • 3 g fat • 80 kcal

  • 2 cups lauki, peeled and cubed
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp olive oil or butter
  • 2 cups water or vegetable stock
  • Salt, pepper, ¼ tsp cumin powder, pinch of nutmeg

Method: Heat oil, sauté onion and garlic until soft. Add lauki cubes and cook for 2 minutes. Add water/stock, salt, and cumin. Simmer for 15 minutes until lauki is very tender. Blend until smooth. Return to pot, adjust seasoning, add pepper and nutmeg. Serve hot with a squeeze of lemon. Serves 2.

10. Methi Thepla

Approx. per serving (2 theplas): 30 g carbs • 6 g protein • 5 g fat • 190 kcal

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour (or ½ wheat + ½ bajra flour)
  • 1 cup chopped fresh methi (fenugreek) leaves
  • 1 tbsp curd, ½ tsp turmeric, ½ tsp red chilli powder
  • ½ tsp ajwain (carom seeds), salt, 1 tsp oil in dough
  • Water as needed

Method: Mix flour, methi leaves, curd, all spices, salt, and oil. Knead into a semi-soft dough using minimal water. Rest for 10 minutes. Divide into small balls, roll into thin circles. Cook on a hot tawa with minimal oil until golden spots appear on both sides. Serve with curd or green chutney. Makes 6–8 theplas.

🥜 Snack Recipes

11. Roasted Chana (Chickpeas)

Approx. per serving (30 g): 12 g carbs • 6 g protein • 2 g fat • 110 kcal

Toss dried kala chana or canned chickpeas (drained) with ½ tsp olive oil, salt, chaat masala, and red chilli powder. Roast in the oven at 180°C for 25–30 minutes, stirring halfway, until crunchy. Store in an airtight container for up to a week.

12. Sprout Salad

Approx. per serving: 14 g carbs • 8 g protein • 1 g fat • 95 kcal

  • 1 cup mixed sprouts (moong, moth, chana)
  • ¼ cup each: chopped cucumber, tomato, onion
  • Juice of ½ lemon, salt, ½ tsp chaat masala, fresh coriander
  • 1 green chilli, finely chopped (optional)

Method: Steam sprouts for 5 minutes (optional — can be eaten raw if fresh). Mix all ingredients. Serve immediately. Serves 1.

13. Cucumber Raita

Approx. per serving: 8 g carbs • 5 g protein • 2 g fat • 70 kcal

  • 1 cup low-fat curd (dahi)
  • ½ cup grated cucumber, squeezed dry
  • ¼ tsp roasted cumin powder, pinch of black salt
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped

Method: Whisk curd until smooth. Mix in cucumber, cumin, salt, and mint. Chill before serving. Serves 2.

14. Roasted Makhana (Fox Nuts)

Approx. per serving (1 cup): 15 g carbs • 4 g protein • 2 g fat • 90 kcal

  • 2 cups makhana (fox nuts / lotus seeds)
  • 1 tsp ghee or olive oil
  • Salt, ¼ tsp black pepper, ¼ tsp turmeric
  • Optional: ¼ tsp chaat masala or peri-peri seasoning

Method: Heat ghee in a pan on low flame. Add makhana and roast on low-medium heat for 7–8 minutes, stirring continuously, until they become crunchy (they will feel light and crisp). Add spices, toss well, and let cool. Store in an airtight container. Serves 2.

💡 Recipe Tips for Diabetes

  • Use minimal oil — invest in good non-stick cookware. Aim for 2–3 tsp of oil per meal for the entire cooking.
  • Replace sugar with stevia or monk fruit sweetener in recipes that need sweetness.
  • Add vegetables to everything — extra veggies in upma, poha, dal, and even dosa batter increase fibre and lower GI.
  • Use whole spices generously — they add flavour without calories or carbs.
  • Cook rice with a tsp of coconut oil and refrigerate for 12 hours before reheating. This increases resistant starch, lowering the effective GI by up to 10–15%.
  • Pair carbs with protein — always include a protein source alongside carbohydrate-rich foods.
Batch-cook dals, roasted chana, and makhana on weekends so healthy options are always ready during busy weekdays.
