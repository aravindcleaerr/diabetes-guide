💊 GLP-1 Receptor Agonist Advances

Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1 RAs) have transformed Type 2 diabetes management. These drugs mimic the incretin hormone GLP-1, stimulating glucose-dependent insulin secretion, suppressing glucagon release, slowing gastric emptying, and promoting satiety.

Semaglutide

  • Subcutaneous (Ozempic): Once-weekly injection; HbA1c reductions of 1.5 – 1.8% in clinical trials (SUSTAIN programme). Demonstrated significant cardiovascular risk reduction (SELECT trial — 20% reduction in major adverse cardiovascular events).
  • Oral (Rybelsus): First oral GLP-1 RA; 7 mg and 14 mg tablets taken daily on an empty stomach. Comparable efficacy to many injectable GLP-1 RAs.
  • High-dose for obesity (Wegovy): 2.4 mg weekly; average weight loss of ~15% body weight, with cardiovascular outcome benefits confirmed in the SELECT trial.

Tirzepatide (Mounjaro / Zepbound)

  • Dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist — the first in its class.
  • SURPASS trials showed HbA1c reductions up to 2.4% and weight loss up to 12.4 kg (~25 lbs) at the highest dose.
  • SURMOUNT-1 obesity trial: up to 22.5% body weight reduction.
  • Cardiovascular outcome data (SURPASS-CVOT) further supports long-term safety and benefit.
Note: GLP-1 RAs are not a substitute for lifestyle changes. They work best when combined with a balanced diet and regular physical activity.

🧬 SGLT2 Inhibitors — Cardiovascular & Renal Benefits

Sodium-glucose co-transporter 2 inhibitors (SGLT2i) block glucose reabsorption in the proximal tubule of the kidney, promoting glycosuria and lowering blood glucose. Their benefits extend far beyond glycaemic control.

Proven Benefits Beyond Blood Sugar

  • Heart failure: Reduced hospitalisations for heart failure by 25–35%, effective in both reduced and preserved ejection fraction — now approved for heart failure even without diabetes.
  • Kidney protection: Slowed progression of chronic kidney disease (CKD) by 30–40%, reduced albuminuria, delayed need for dialysis. DAPA-CKD and EMPA-KIDNEY trials were stopped early due to overwhelming benefit.
  • Cardiovascular mortality: EMPA-REG OUTCOME demonstrated a 38% relative risk reduction in cardiovascular death with empagliflozin.
Warning: SGLT2 inhibitors carry a risk of euglycaemic diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA), genital mycotic infections, and urinary tract infections. They should be paused during acute illness, surgery, or prolonged fasting.

🤖 Closed-Loop Insulin Systems (Artificial Pancreas)

Closed-loop systems, also called automated insulin delivery (AID) or hybrid closed-loop systems, combine a continuous glucose monitor (CGM), an insulin pump, and a control algorithm to automate basal insulin delivery with minimal user input.

How They Work

  • The CGM reads interstitial glucose every 1–5 minutes.
  • An algorithm (running on the pump or a smartphone) predicts glucose trends and adjusts basal insulin delivery in real time.
  • Users still bolus for meals in most hybrid systems; fully closed-loop systems aim to eliminate this step.

🔬 Islet Cell Transplantation Research

Islet transplantation involves infusing isolated clusters of insulin-producing beta cells (islets of Langerhans) from a donor pancreas into the recipient's liver via the portal vein.

Current Status

  • Edmonton Protocol (2000): Landmark procedure using steroid-free immunosuppression. Demonstrated insulin independence in Type 1 diabetes, but most recipients returned to some insulin use within 5 years.
  • FDA approval (2023): Donislecel (Lantidra) became the first FDA-approved allogeneic islet cell therapy for Type 1 diabetes patients with severe hypoglycaemia unawareness unresponsive to standard management.
  • Limitations: Requires lifelong immunosuppression, limited donor supply, gradual graft loss over time.

Encapsulation Strategies

Researchers are developing biocompatible capsules or devices that shield transplanted islets from immune attack, potentially eliminating the need for immunosuppressive drugs. Approaches include macro-encapsulation devices and hydrogel micro-capsules with engineered porosity.

🧪 Stem Cell Therapy Trials

Stem cell-derived beta cell replacement aims to solve the donor shortage problem that limits islet transplantation.

Key Programmes

  • Vertex Pharmaceuticals (VX-880 / VX-264): VX-880 uses stem cell-derived, fully differentiated islet cells infused via the portal vein with immunosuppression. Early-phase results showed patients achieving insulin independence. VX-264 encapsulates the same cells in an immunoprotective device to avoid immunosuppression.
  • ViaCyte (now part of Vertex): Pioneered the PEC-Direct and PEC-Encap programmes using pancreatic progenitor cells in implantable devices.
  • Sernova: Cell Pouch system — a pre-vascularised subcutaneous pouch that creates a favourable environment for transplanted islets or stem cell-derived cells.
Note: While results are promising, stem cell therapies are still in clinical trials and not yet widely available. Challenges include scalable manufacturing, long-term graft survival, and immune tolerance.

🩹 Smart Insulin Patches

Smart insulin patches are microneedle-based transdermal patches designed to deliver insulin automatically in response to rising blood glucose levels, mimicking a healthy pancreas.

How They Work

  • The patch contains an array of tiny, painless microneedles loaded with insulin housed in glucose-responsive materials.
  • When blood glucose rises, the glucose-responsive matrix (e.g., phenylboronic acid, glucose oxidase-based systems) undergoes a structural change, releasing insulin into the interstitial fluid.
  • As glucose normalises, insulin release slows or stops, reducing hypoglycaemia risk.

Research Highlights

  • Pre-clinical studies in animal models have demonstrated rapid glucose normalisation within 30 minutes lasting several hours.
  • Multiple academic groups (e.g., Zhen Gu lab at Zhejiang University / UCLA) and start-ups are advancing patch prototypes toward human trials.
  • Challenges remain in dose precision, shelf stability, wearability, and regulatory approval for human use.
Tip: Smart patches could one day offer a painless, needle-free, set-and-forget alternative to injections and pumps — but they remain in the early research phase.

💉 Once-Weekly Insulins

Insulin Icodec (Awiqli)

Insulin icodec is a basal insulin analogue designed for once-weekly subcutaneous injection, developed by Novo Nordisk.

  • Mechanism: Strong, reversible binding to albumin gives icodec a half-life of approximately one week, enabling steady-state glucose-lowering with a single weekly injection.
  • ONWARDS trial programme: Six phase 3a trials across Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes demonstrated non-inferior HbA1c reduction compared to once-daily basal insulins (glargine U100/U300, degludec).
  • Benefits: Reduces injection burden from 365 to 52 injections per year; may improve adherence and quality of life, particularly in patients reluctant to start insulin.
  • Considerations: Slightly higher rates of mild hypoglycaemia observed in some trials; dose adjustments take longer to take effect due to the long half-life; requires careful planning around illness or procedures.
