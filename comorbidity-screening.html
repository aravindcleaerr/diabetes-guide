Comorbidity Screening

Annual screening checklist to catch complications early and stay ahead of diabetes

🔍 Why Routine Screening Matters

Diabetes affects virtually every organ system over time. Many complications develop silently — by the time symptoms appear, significant damage may already have occurred. Regular screening allows early detection and intervention, dramatically improving long-term outcomes.

The American Diabetes Association (ADA) and RSSDI (India) both recommend a structured annual screening protocol for all people with diabetes. The table below consolidates their guidelines.

📝 How to Use This Checklist

  • Print or save this page and bring it to your annual review appointment
  • Tick off each screening as it is completed and note the date and result
  • If any test is overdue, flag it with your doctor at the next visit
  • Store all results in a single folder (physical or digital) for easy reference
Many hospitals in India now offer "diabetes annual health check" packages that bundle most of these tests at a discounted rate. Ask your hospital or lab about such packages.

📊 Understanding Your Results

HbA1c Interpretation

  • Below 5.7%: Normal
  • 5.7% – 6.4%: Prediabetes
  • 6.5% and above: Diabetes
  • Target for most diabetics: Below 7% (individualised)

Kidney Function (eGFR Stages)

  • Stage 1 (eGFR ≥ 90): Normal kidney function
  • Stage 2 (eGFR 60–89): Mildly reduced
  • Stage 3a (eGFR 45–59): Mildly to moderately reduced
  • Stage 3b (eGFR 30–44): Moderately to severely reduced
  • Stage 4 (eGFR 15–29): Severely reduced
  • Stage 5 (eGFR < 15): Kidney failure
If your eGFR drops below 60 or UACR rises above 30 mg/g, your doctor will likely increase the frequency of kidney monitoring and may adjust your medications. Some diabetes drugs (like metformin) need dose adjustment or discontinuation in advanced kidney disease.
