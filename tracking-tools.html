📈 Why Tracking Matters

Diabetes management is data-driven. The more consistently you track your glucose, food, exercise, and medications, the better you and your doctor can identify patterns, adjust treatment, and prevent complications. Modern tools make this easier than ever.

📓 Blood Glucose Logbooks

The simplest tracking method is a paper logbook or a printed spreadsheet. Record the date, time, glucose reading, meal context (fasting, pre-meal, post-meal), and any notes (sick day, exercise, stress). Many glucometer manufacturers provide free logbooks.

Even if you use an app, keeping a paper backup for the first few weeks helps build the habit and serves as a fallback.

📟 Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGM) and Data Analysis

CGMs like FreeStyle Libre, Dexcom G6/G7, and Medtronic Guardian provide glucose readings every 1–5 minutes, generating a rich dataset. Key metrics to review:

  • Time in Range (TIR): Percentage of time glucose is between 70–180 mg/dL — target > 70%
  • Time below range: Target < 4% below 70 mg/dL
  • Time above range: Target < 25% above 180 mg/dL
  • Glucose Management Indicator (GMI): Estimated HbA1c from CGM data
  • Coefficient of Variation (CV): Measure of glucose variability — target < 36%
  • Ambulatory Glucose Profile (AGP): A standardised report showing daily glucose patterns
CGM companion apps (LibreLink, Dexcom Clarity, CareLink) generate detailed reports that you can share with your doctor electronically or as PDFs.

🍎 Food Tracking Apps

  • MyFitnessPal: Largest food database; barcode scanner; tracks calories, carbs, protein, fat
  • HealthifyMe: Indian food database with Indian meals and recipes; Hindi support
  • YAZIO: Clean interface with meal planning features
  • Carbs & Cals: Photo-based portion guide — excellent for visual learners

⌚ Fitness Trackers and Smart Devices

  • Step counters: Target 7,000–10,000 steps/day; any wearable (Fitbit, Apple Watch, Mi Band) works
  • Heart rate monitors: Useful for tracking exercise intensity and detecting irregular heartbeat
  • Smart scales: Track weight trends over time; some measure body composition (fat %, muscle mass)
  • Blood pressure monitors: Home BP monitors (Omron, Dr. Morepen) with app connectivity for trend tracking

👨‍⚕️ Sharing Data with Your Doctor

  • Most apps can generate PDF reports — email these to your doctor before appointments
  • CGM platforms (LibreView, Dexcom Clarity) allow you to share access directly with your clinic
  • Bring your glucose meter to every appointment — doctors can download data directly
  • Highlight any patterns you have noticed (e.g., "glucose always spikes after breakfast")
  • Be honest in your logging — accurate data leads to better treatment decisions
Set a weekly reminder to review your own data. Look for patterns: Are you consistently high after a particular meal? Do you go low during afternoon exercise? These insights are powerful.
