🏛️ RSSDI — Research Society for the Study of Diabetes in India

  • India's largest professional body for diabetes care and research
  • Publishes Indian diabetes management guidelines (RSSDI Clinical Practice Recommendations)
  • Organises annual conferences and continuing medical education
  • Website: rssdi.in — useful for finding registered diabetologists near you
  • Has state-level chapters across India

📞 Diabetes Helplines

  • RSSDI Diabetes Helpline: Check rssdi.in for current contact numbers
  • Apollo 24/7: 1860-500-1066 (teleconsult and health queries)
  • Practo: In-app chat and video consultation with endocrinologists
  • BeatO: Diabetes-specific helpline via their app
  • iCall (TISS): 9152987821 — free counselling for mental health and chronic disease distress
  • Vandrevala Foundation: 1860-2662-345 — mental health support helpline (24/7)

🇮🇳 Government Programs

NPCDCS (National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, CVD and Stroke)

  • Free screening for diabetes at district and sub-district hospitals
  • Free or subsidised medications (metformin, glimepiride, insulin) at government health centres
  • NCD clinics at Community Health Centres (CHCs) and District Hospitals
  • Population-based screening camps in urban and rural areas

Jan Aushadhi Kendras (Affordable Medicines)

  • Government-run generic medicine stores offering medications at 50–90% discount
  • Over 9,000 stores across India
  • Diabetes medications available: metformin, glimepiride, glipizide, pioglitazone, insulin (human), test strips
  • Find nearest store: janaushadhi.gov.in

Ayushman Bharat — Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY)

  • Free hospitalisation cover of Rs 5 lakh per family per year
  • Covers diabetes-related hospitalisations, surgeries (amputation, cardiac procedures), dialysis
  • For BPL and vulnerable families — check eligibility at pmjay.gov.in
  • Does not cover outpatient medications (you still need to buy daily diabetes medicines)

Ayushman Bharat Health and Wellness Centres (AB-HWC)

  • Primary care centres providing free NCD screening and basic management
  • Over 1,50,000 centres operational across India
  • Free BP and glucose testing

💉 Affordable Insulin Sources

  • Jan Aushadhi stores: Human insulin (regular and NPH) at significantly reduced prices
  • Government hospital pharmacies: Free insulin for BPL card holders at many state-run hospitals
  • Novo Nordisk patient assistance: Programs for patients who cannot afford insulin — ask your doctor
  • Biocon: Indian manufacturer of biosimilar insulin glargine (Basalog) at lower cost than originator brands
  • Wockhardt and Lupin: Also manufacture affordable insulin in India
If insulin cost is a barrier, talk to your endocrinologist. There are almost always more affordable alternatives or assistance programs available.

📍 Bangalore-Specific Resources

Hospitals with Strong Diabetes Departments

  • Manipal Hospital (Old Airport Road, Whitefield, Sarjapur Road): Dedicated endocrinology department; insulin pump and CGM services; diabetes educator on staff
  • Narayana Health (Bommasandra): Affordable multi-speciality care; diabetes and endocrinology clinic; Narayana Hrudayalaya for cardiac care
  • Apollo Hospital (Bannerghatta Road, Jayanagar): Comprehensive diabetes centre; Apollo Sugar clinics for dedicated diabetes care
  • Jnana Sanjeevini Diabetes Centre (Rajarajeshwari Nagar): Dedicated diabetes hospital with education programs
  • Fortis Hospital (Bannerghatta Road, Cunningham Road): Endocrinology and metabolic diseases department
  • M.S. Ramaiah Hospital (New BEL Road): Teaching hospital with endocrinology department; affordable care

Diabetes Educators and Support Groups in Bangalore

  • Diabetes India (Bangalore chapter): Regular awareness programs and patient education events
  • Apollo Sugar Clinics: Chain of dedicated diabetes management clinics with certified diabetes educators
  • Local walking groups: Cubbon Park, Lalbagh, and Sankey Tank morning walker groups — great for peer support and exercise
  • Online support: Facebook group "Diabetes Support India" and WhatsApp groups (ask your hospital's diabetes educator)
Many Bangalore hospitals organise free diabetes awareness camps during World Diabetes Day (November 14) and throughout the year. Follow their social media pages for announcements.
