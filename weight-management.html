📏 Waist Circumference — A Better Indicator

Abdominal (visceral) fat is more metabolically harmful than overall body fat. Waist circumference is a practical proxy for visceral fat.

How to measure: Stand upright, find the midpoint between the lowest rib and the top of the hip bone. Wrap a measuring tape around the waist at this level, keeping it snug but not compressing the skin. Measure at the end of a normal exhalation.

🔥 Caloric Deficit Basics

Weight loss occurs when you consistently consume fewer calories than you burn. This is called a caloric deficit.

  • 1 kg of body fat ≈ 7,700 calories.
  • A deficit of 500 calories/day leads to approximately 0.5 kg (1 lb) weight loss per week.
  • A deficit of 500–750 calories/day is considered safe and sustainable.
  • Calculate your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) using online calculators or a dietitian assessment, then subtract 500–750 to get your target intake.

Where to Create the Deficit

  • Diet alone: Easier to create a 500-calorie deficit by cutting food than by exercising it off. However, diet-only approaches risk muscle loss.
  • Exercise alone: Difficult to sustain a large deficit through exercise alone (a 30-minute brisk walk burns only ~150 calories).
  • Combination (recommended): Reduce intake by ~300 calories and increase activity to burn ~200 calories. Preserves muscle and is more sustainable.

📈 The Impact of Weight Loss on Diabetes

Weight loss is one of the most powerful interventions for Type 2 diabetes. The benefits are dose-dependent:

Weight LossExpected Benefits
5% of body weightImproved insulin sensitivity; modest reduction in fasting glucose and HbA1c (0.2–0.3%)
7–10%Significant improvement in HbA1c (0.5–1.0%); reduced blood pressure; improved lipid profile; potential reduction or elimination of some medications
10–15%Potential diabetes remission (HbA1c <6.5% without glucose-lowering medications); substantially reduced cardiovascular risk
>15% (often via surgery)High rates of diabetes remission (up to 60–80%); major improvements in sleep apnoea, joint health, and quality of life
For a person weighing 90 kg, even a 5% loss (4.5 kg) produces meaningful metabolic improvements. Set initial goals that are achievable — perfection is not required.

🎯 Sustainable Weight Loss Strategies

  • Focus on behaviour change, not just numbers: Build habits you can maintain for years, not just weeks.
  • Prioritise protein: Aim for 1.0–1.2 g/kg/day of protein. Protein preserves muscle during weight loss, increases satiety, and has the highest thermic effect of food.
  • Eat more fibre: 25–30 g/day from vegetables, legumes, and whole grains increases fullness.
  • Reduce liquid calories: Eliminate sugary drinks, fruit juices, and alcohol (or limit to minimal).
  • Practise mindful eating: Eat slowly, without screens, paying attention to hunger and fullness cues.
  • Get adequate sleep: 7–8 hours. Sleep deprivation increases ghrelin (hunger hormone) and decreases leptin (satiety hormone).
  • Manage stress: Chronic stress elevates cortisol, which promotes visceral fat storage and insulin resistance.
  • Track food intake: People who log food consistently lose 2× more weight than those who don’t. Use apps like MyFitnessPal, HealthifyMe, or a simple diary.
  • Regular physical activity: Both aerobic and resistance training. Exercise is especially critical for maintaining weight loss long-term.
  • Structured programmes: The Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) lifestyle intervention (aimed at 7% weight loss + 150 min exercise/week) reduced diabetes risk by 58%.

⚠ Avoiding Crash Diets

Why crash diets are harmful in diabetes:
  • Muscle loss: Very low calorie diets (<800 kcal/day) cause significant muscle loss, worsening insulin resistance.
  • Hypoglycemia risk: Dramatic calorie restriction while on diabetes medications (especially insulin or sulfonylureas) can cause dangerous low blood sugar.
  • Metabolic slowdown: Severe restriction lowers basal metabolic rate, making long-term maintenance harder.
  • Nutrient deficiencies: Restricting food groups can lead to deficiencies in vitamins, minerals, and essential fatty acids.
  • Rebound weight gain: 80–95% of people who lose weight rapidly regain it within 1–2 years, often with additional weight.
  • Gallstones: Rapid weight loss (>1.5 kg/week) increases gallstone risk.

Approaches to be cautious about: juice cleanses, detox diets, single-food diets, extreme keto without medical supervision, any plan promising >1 kg/week loss.

Very Low Calorie Diets (VLCDs, 800 kcal/day) are sometimes used under strict medical supervision for short periods (8–12 weeks) to initiate rapid weight loss. This must only be done under a doctor’s care with medication adjustments.

🏭 Bariatric / Metabolic Surgery

For people with severe obesity and poorly controlled diabetes, bariatric surgery is the most effective long-term treatment, often producing diabetes remission.

Eligibility Criteria (Asian Indian thresholds)

  • BMI ≥37.5 regardless of diabetes control.
  • BMI 32.5–37.4 with inadequately controlled Type 2 diabetes despite optimal medical therapy.
  • BMI 27.5–32.4: May be considered on a case-by-case basis if diabetes is uncontrolled with optimal medical therapy (emerging evidence).
These thresholds are lower than Western guidelines (BMI ≥40 or ≥35 with comorbidities) because Asian Indians develop metabolic complications at lower BMI values.

Important Considerations

  • Surgery is a tool, not a cure — lifelong dietary changes, exercise, and follow-up are essential.
  • Lifelong vitamin and mineral supplementation is required (B12, iron, calcium, vitamin D, folate).
  • Diabetes medications often need to be reduced or stopped immediately after surgery to prevent hypoglycemia.
  • Some patients experience diabetes recurrence after 5–10 years; long-term follow-up is crucial.

💉 GLP-1 Receptor Agonists for Weight Loss

GLP-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1 RAs) are injectable medications originally developed for diabetes that also produce significant weight loss by reducing appetite and slowing gastric emptying.

MedicationAverage Weight LossDiabetes BenefitAdministration
Liraglutide (Saxenda/Victoza)5–8% of body weightLowers HbA1c by 1–1.5%Daily injection
Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy)10–15%Lowers HbA1c by 1.5–2%Weekly injection
Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound)15–22%Lowers HbA1c by 2–2.5%Weekly injection
  • These medications address both diabetes and obesity simultaneously — a major advantage.
  • Common side effects: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (usually improve over weeks).
  • Weight regain occurs if medication is stopped — long-term use may be needed.
  • Contraindicated in personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2 syndrome.
  • Cost can be a barrier; availability varies by country.
GLP-1 RAs are increasingly seen as a bridge or complement to lifestyle changes. They can help break through weight loss plateaus and are now considered first-line for overweight/obese patients with Type 2 diabetes by many guidelines.

📝 Key Takeaways

  • Even modest weight loss (5–10%) significantly improves diabetes control.
  • Use Asian Indian BMI and waist circumference cut-offs for accurate risk assessment.
  • Combine dietary changes with exercise for sustainable weight loss.
  • Avoid crash diets — they are dangerous in diabetes and unsustainable.
  • Bariatric surgery is an effective option for eligible patients with severe obesity.
  • GLP-1 receptor agonists offer pharmacological support for weight loss alongside glucose lowering.
  • Long-term success requires permanent lifestyle changes, not temporary diets.
